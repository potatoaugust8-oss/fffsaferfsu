Privacy and Ethics

Privacy isn‚Äôt optional‚Äîit‚Äôs built in from the start.

1) Data Minimization

  • Encrypted biometric template, not raw image.
  • Timestamped access events.
  • Anonymous ID tag (no personal identifiers stored with templates).

2) Security Measures

  • Encryption at rest and in transit.
  • Role‚Äëbased access control for administrators.
  • Regular audits of who accesses data and why.

3) Consent and Control

  • Participation is opt‚Äëin.
  • Students can unenroll anytime; templates are deleted within 24 hours.
  • Clear explanations at every step.

4) Transparency and Oversight

  • Annual transparency reports published online.
  • Student‚ÄìFaculty Oversight Board ensures compliance.
  • Independent privacy audits verify system integrity.

5) Fairness and Accessibility

  • Testing for bias across demographics.
  • Alternatives for those whose biometrics don‚Äôt work reliably.

FSU Rollout Plan

Structured phases with clear success metrics and rollback triggers.

Phase 1: Pilot (Fall)

  • Locations: One dorm, one computer lab, and Leach Center turnstiles.
  • Enrollment: 100% voluntary.
  • Alternatives: NFC cards available at every pilot site.
  • Evaluation: Efficiency, satisfaction, bias testing, and privacy metrics.
  • Reporting: Monthly transparency updates.

Phase 2: Evaluation (Winter)

  • Independent audit of privacy safeguards.
  • Adjust retention periods and policies based on student feedback.
  • Upgrade to on‚Äëdevice verification where possible.

Phase 3: Expansion (Spring)

  • Only if pilot success metrics are met.
  • Oversight board vote required before expansion.
  • Continued opt‚Äëin participation and full alternative options.
