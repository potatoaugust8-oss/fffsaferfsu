Protecting Students, Preserving Privacy

This advocacy site promotes a privacy‑preserving biometric access system at Florida State University. It emphasizes safety, convenience, and ethical use of data to protect the student body while enhancing campus access systems.

Why Biometrics?

Current card‑based entry systems are easy to lose or share, reducing security. A biometric system ensures that only the authorized person gains access, making campus life both safer and more efficient. The design protects privacy through encrypted templates rather than raw images.

Privacy & Ethics

  • Encrypted biometric templates, never raw images.
  • Opt‑in participation with full transparency.
  • Annual audits to ensure privacy and fairness.

FSU Rollout Plan

Pilot programs begin in selected dorms and facilities with voluntary enrollment, supported by NFC card alternatives. Performance and privacy audits determine future expansion.

Get Involved

Students and faculty can sign support letters, join the feedback team, or participate in pilot testing. Contact us with your feedback.
